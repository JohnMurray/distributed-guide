A Guide Book for Distributed Computation

Distributed computation (programming) can be a difficult and nebulous topic to learn. This book introduces concepts in distributed computing as well as foundational topics and resources for further learning. This is the result of self-teachings and content, as it stands today, is very much a work-in-progress. Read online for free and eventually I'll get some e-book versions up here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why?

I've long had an interest in distributed computing, but it can be hard to know where to look and what to read to really get started. I've recently told myself that I'm going to get more serious about learning this topic and I wanted to put together a guide based on what I learn (starting from nothing, more or less). This guide is intended to be what I wish existed right when I started, so future folks can benifit. It's also a way to help me memorize these concepts and shamelessly promote myself.

How soon can I get an e-book version?

The guide is still very much in the early stages and, given that, there is just a lot up in there air in terms of content, layout, organization, etc. After I collect more information and nail down the particulars, then I'll look at generating and distributing e-book versions.
